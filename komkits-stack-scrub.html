What happens between a sale and a filed return

Five layers, one continuous pipeline. As you scroll, the stack lights up one layer at a time to show where a bill actually goes — same pinned-column mechanism as before, applied to a layered system diagram instead of a slider or a morph.

LAYER 1
Sale & POS capture
Items, quantities, and customer are recorded at the counter or on an order.
LAYER 2
Discount engine
Line-item and header-level discounts are applied and sequenced.
LAYER 3
Tax engine
GST rates resolve per line, respecting inclusive or exclusive pricing.
LAYER 4
Invoice generation
A GST-compliant invoice is composed from the priced, taxed order.
LAYER 5
Filing & reports
Totals roll up into GSTR-ready summaries and business reports.

01 · Capture

Every bill starts at the point of sale

Whether it's rung up on the POS or entered as a sales order, the raw line items are the single source of truth for everything downstream.

02 · Discounting

Discounts apply before tax touches the price

Line-level discounts resolve first, then header-level ones — the order matters, because it changes the taxable value.

This is the sequencing that trips up tax-inclusive pricing if it's done in the wrong order.

03 · Tax

GST resolves per line, not per invoice

Each line can carry its own rate and inclusive/exclusive behavior, so mixed-rate invoices still net out correctly.

04 · Invoicing

The invoice is assembled, not typed

By the time a template renders, every number on it — subtotal, discount, tax, total — has already been decided upstream.

05 · Filing

Nothing is re-entered for the return

The same figures that printed on the invoice are what roll up into the filing summary — one calculation, two destinations.

What this pattern is doing

Same sectionProgress() function as the slider and morph samples — one scroll listener, one 0–1 value per section. Here it drives which .stack-layer gets the active class, so the diagram reads top-to-bottom as a real pipeline instead of an arbitrary set of steps. The numbered layers are justified this time: the sequence is the point, since discount-before-tax ordering is exactly the kind of thing worth making visually explicit.
