# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

The primary visitor is a small-to-mid business owner or operator — the person who actually runs invoicing, inventory, payments, and returns themselves rather than delegating to a finance department. They are non-technical, time-poor, and skeptical of software that adds work instead of removing it. Their context on arrival is a quick evaluation: they want to know, fast, whether this handles the tedious back-office loop they already dread. Secondary visitors are online sellers evaluating the coming Shopify / WooCommerce / WhatsApp integrations, but the page is written and designed to win the owner-operator first.

## Product Purpose

KomKits is a modular, AI-native business-management platform that runs a business's full back office — sales and purchase invoicing, item management, returns, expense and income tracking, payment accounts, and staff/salary — with AI built into the core rather than bolted on. Its purpose is to collapse the tedious operational and reporting work of running a business into one calm place, and to let owners ask questions of their real business data in plain language (via their own AI key, and via a built-in MCP server that connects tools like ChatGPT or Claude). Success is a visitor who feels the weight of admin lift and starts a free workspace.

## Positioning

An AI-native, modular back office that speaks your language and connects to the AI tools you already use — start with the invoicing and inventory you need today, ready for Shopify, WooCommerce, and WhatsApp commerce tomorrow.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: Get Started Free (self-serve signup is the conversion event).
- Secondary CTA: See What's Coming — sends not-yet-ready visitors to the roadmap/integrations rather than losing them.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: "The back office that talks to AI."
- Belief ladder (what an owner-operator must believe, in order, before signing up):
  1. This is a complete back office — invoicing, inventory, payments, returns — not a single-trick tool.
  2. It already covers what I do day-to-day *today*, not just a roadmap of promises.
  3. The AI is real and works with the tools I already use (my own key, MCP) — not a gimmick or an upsell.
  4. It's modular — I can start with only what I need and add more as I grow.
  5. It's credible enough to trust with my business's money.
  6. Starting is free and takes minutes, so there's no risk in trying.

## Brand Personality

Capable, calm, modern. The voice is grounded and reassuring — serious software that feels light. It never shouts, never leans on hype adjectives, and never talks down to a non-technical owner. The emotional target is effortless relief: every section should make the visitor feel that the admin and reporting weight they carry is about to get lighter, not that they're being sold a complicated system. Confidence comes from showing the product work, not from superlatives.

## Anti-references

Grounded in the project's own `komkits-landing/design-research-2026-07.md`, which explicitly rejected these registers:

- Dark dev-tool aesthetic (Vercel, Cursor): dark-mode-default with neon accents reads as "developer tool." KomKits is light and business-facing; that is deliberately not its register.
- Maximalist motion (Framer): kinetic type, cursor-triggered reveals everywhere, audio-on-hover. KomKits holds to roughly one earned motion moment per section, not motion as spectacle.
- Playful 3D illustration (Clay): pastel sculptural blobs are too whimsical for a tool people trust with money.
- Legacy accounting/ERP look (dense gray spreadsheets, QuickBooks/Tally-era chrome): the thing owner-operators are escaping, not returning to.
- Generic AI-SaaS slop: warm cream/parchment body backgrounds, a tiny tracked uppercase eyebrow above every section, and endless identical icon+heading+text card grids.

## Design Principles

- Show the product doing work. Real product UI and AI visibly working beat stock illustration or claims; the page proves rather than asserts.
- Design for effortless relief. Every section should reduce the felt weight of admin, never add visual or cognitive noise; restraint is the feature.
- Monochrome-first; color is meaning. The surface is ink-on-white. Color appears only to carry state — live (green), coming-soon (teal), attention (amber) — never as decoration.
- Practice what you preach. An AI-native product deserves a site that feels intelligent and modern in its own interactions, not a static brochure for a smart tool.
- Modular clarity. Communicate "use only what you need, add as you grow" through structure and pacing, so the platform's core promise is legible from the layout itself.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA: body text at ≥4.5:1 contrast (large text ≥3:1), full keyboard operability with visible focus states, and a `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative for every animation (the mockup already establishes a global reduced-motion path). Given the non-technical, broad SMB audience, favor plain language over jargon and generous target sizes.
